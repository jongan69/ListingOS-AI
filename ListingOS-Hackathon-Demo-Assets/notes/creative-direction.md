# ListingOS Demo Creative Direction

Scope: the **Track B overlay layer** — device mockups, text cards, typography, colour, and
music. Track A (live footage and on-camera beats) is governed by
[`../PRODUCTION_PLAN.md`](../PRODUCTION_PLAN.md).

Note: sections below referencing "the 30s and 60s versions" predate the current single
~2:53 cut. Read those as "the opener" and "the hero beat" respectively; the template and
music picks themselves are still current.

## Visual Goal

Make ListingOS feel like a premium mobile product launch:

- dark, glossy, image-led
- fast and intentional
- readable at a glance
- confident without feeling fake

The footage should emphasize speed, simplicity, and seller utility over technical novelty.

## Rotato Template Picks

### Primary Hero Picks

1. `Laptop-Phone Bundle/Phone Pop Up.rotatotemplate`
   - Best use: opening hero shot for the 30s and 60s versions
   - Reason: quick, legible, premium single-device reveal

2. `Laptop-Phone Bundle/Focus on Floating.rotatotemplate`
   - Best use: closer product beauty shot after the hook
   - Reason: keeps the app front and center without too much motion clutter

3. `Laptop-Phone Bundle/Pullback to Floating Phone.rotatotemplate`
   - Best use: ending brand lockup or transition into final CTA
   - Reason: gives a polished pullback that feels like a launch close

### Secondary Picks

1. `Laptop-Phone Bundle/Slide in Mobile.rotatotemplate`
   - Best use: transition clip between problem statement and app proof

2. `Mega-Multi 1.0/4 phones carousel app store.rotato`
   - Best use: optional montage if multiple screens are captured cleanly
   - Risk: easier to become visually noisy, so use only if readability stays strong

3. `Mega-Multi 1.0/Phones stacked.rotato`
   - Best use: optional fast-cut social version
   - Risk: less ideal for the main Devpost cut because the UI can become too small

## Rotato Avoid List

- Templates that shrink the device too aggressively
- Multi-phone layouts where the UI becomes unreadable
- Laptop-dominant compositions for the main proof sequence
- Web templates unless we deliberately use a web surface clip

## Music Pick

**Rights gate:** the repository contains candidate audio files, not proof of a license.
Confirm the exact track's ownership or license before publication and retain that evidence.
A filename containing `no-copyright` is not sufficient. If rights cannot be confirmed,
replace the bed with owned or clearly licensed audio before the final Devpost lock.

## Edit Rhythm

- first 3 seconds: big claim + strongest app reveal
- next 10 to 15 seconds: product promise and photo intake
- middle: AI + review proof
- close: listing-ready or published proof + brand

## Typography / Caption Feel

- concise captions
- no paragraph blocks
- one punchy idea per beat
- keep words short enough to read on mobile

## Color / Finish Notes

- preserve the app's dark glassy look
- avoid over-warming the footage
- keep contrast high enough that white UI text remains crisp
- device mockups should feel premium, not gimmicky

## Truth Guardrails

- If fresh live publish is not captured, do not stage the edit as if it was.
- If the strongest truthful proof is listing-ready plus prior published proof, structure the edit around that.
- The goal is credibility first, polish second.
